Risk Modeling Paradigm

Hazard

Our concept of hazard differentiates our work from current industry solutions by including land cover in our definition of flood hazard. Research identifies the general trend of converting green spaces to high density urban land use as a significant driver in the magnitude of flood damage.

Science Direct

Different categories of land cover react to water in different ways. The general shift from green spaces to high density urban development has resulted in land cover that does a poor job at both absorbing water and returning it to the water table. Land cover that supports tree canopies, grass, and soil contribute to reducing the effect of storm surge by absorbing a portion of that water while buildings, roads, and other impervious surfaces merely retain flood water. This permits more water to remain in a neighborhood as potential storms and floods continue to pile on, which increases the hazard to a neighborhood.
